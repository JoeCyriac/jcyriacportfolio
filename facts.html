Project 3 Analysis



Description:


This is an analysis of 3 interesting facts I found while working with the data and my predictions for the future regarding Project 3.


  • In 1985, Colorado had negative value for Nuclear Energy (This was due to the state producing more than it used)
  • The numbers for petroleum in Hawaii are very high.
  • The usage of Nuclear Energy grew exponentially after the 1960s, before there was little to none reported.

Analysis:


After completing the project and checking the data, I was shocked by the surge in usage for all 5 types of energy in the database. Seeing how much each state relies on these resources really made me think about what would happen if access to one or more was lost. Thankfully, usage of renewable energy has been growing as time progresses too. Something I would like to see in the future is how these numbers have changed due to the pandemic. Like many other areas, I am sure many of the numbers have changed drastically since the start of the pandemic, most likely for the worse. Another area I believe has changed as a result of the pandemic is sustainability. Just like the numbers for energy potentially changing for the worse, the strive for sustainability in all aspects of our lives has changed. The University of St. Thomas is committed to making sustainability the heart of its mission. As a student, I have seen firsthand that the pandemic has not slowed them down from achieving this mission. This university has shown that even during unprecedented times, they are adaptable and have moved forward in their progression.
